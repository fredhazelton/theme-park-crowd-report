# 🚂 CDR Intel Briefing — 2026-03-14 (TASK-7)

## Canadian Rail Infrastructure Spending Overview

### Alto High-Speed Rail — The Big One
- **Project:** Toronto–Québec City High-Speed Rail Network (formerly HFR)
- **Operator:** Alto (federal Crown corporation), private partner: Cadence
- **Route:** ~1,000 km, Toronto → Ottawa → Montreal → Québec City
- **Speed:** 300+ km/h
- **Design phase budget:** $3.9B CAD (4-5 year design phase)
- **Economic impact:** Up to $35B GDP boost, 51,000 construction jobs, 5,000 operational jobs
- **Construction start:** 2029-2030
- **First phase:** Ottawa–Montreal segment

### BREAKING: Market Sounding Launched (Mar 11)
- **What:** Alto and Cadence launched a phased market-sounding process for major works procurement
- **Phase 1 focus:** Rolling stock and signalling systems
- **Activities:** Meeting manufacturers and suppliers in Canada and internationally
- **Topics:** Technical requirements, timelines, procurement structure, trainset design for Canadian weather, accessibility
- **Previous engagement:** Steel sector consultations started autumn 2025
- **CDR relevance:** This is the procurement phase where digital rail technology providers get their foot in the door. Market sounding = they're accepting industry input NOW

### International Comparisons
- **California HSR:** Still under construction, frequently referenced as cautionary tale
- **UK HS2:** Partially cancelled (Birmingham–Manchester leg), massive cost overruns
- **Spain AVE / Japan Shinkansen / France TGV:** Gold standards for operational HSR
- **CDR angle:** International project comparisons = content that positions us as domain experts
