- 40% of SSD dataset (2,390/5,919 districts) share identical date combos with 50+ others across different states
- Top combo: 284 districts across 23 states with IDENTICAL calendars — clearly hallucinated by LLM scraper
- LLM scraper reporting "found" when it should say "not found" — fabricating plausible dates
- 96.7% hit rate is inflated — real accuracy much lower
- 57% of districts share dates with 20+ others
- SSD quality validator built: 52% flagged, quality score 0.48
- Files: ssd_quality_check.py, run_quality_gate.py, SSD_QUALITY_VALIDATION.md

### The Three Ideas Rule + Decision Learning
- Before escalating a problem to Fred, agents MUST come with 3 realistic ideas to fix it
- Fred picks the best option (or rejects all and asks for more)
- The QA loop: Detect → Diagnose → Propose 3 fixes → Fred decides → Execute → Verify
- Fred is only needed at the decision step — everything else is autonomous
- **Decision learning:** Track which option Fred picks and why. Over time, use patterns to predict his preference.
- Short term: rank options by predicted preference. Medium term: auto-apply predicted choice for routine issues, inform Fred. Long term: only escalate genuinely novel problems.
- Like the Gazoo rule: "complain with solutions"
- Decision log created at `data/decision_log.json`
- Framework updated: FRAMEWORK.md + GUARDRAILS.md
