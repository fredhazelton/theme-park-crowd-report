# Hazeydata Business Setup Checklist
*Sole Proprietorship — Ontario, Canada*

---

## ✅ Immediate Setup

### 1. Business Name Registration
- [ ] Register "Hazeydata" as a business name with Ontario
- **Where:** [Ontario Business Registry](https://www.ontario.ca/page/register-business-name-online)
- **Cost:** ~$60
- **Why:** Legally required if operating under a name other than your personal name
- **Note:** Check if "Hazeydata" is available first

### 2. CRA Business Number (BN)
- [ ] Register for a Business Number with CRA
- **Where:** [CRA Business Registration Online](https://www.canada.ca/en/revenue-agency/services/tax/businesses/topics/registering-your-business/register.html)
- **Cost:** Free
- **Why:** Needed for HST registration, tax filings, and looks professional
- **You'll get:** A 9-digit BN (e.g., 123456789)

### 3. Separate Business Bank Account
- [ ] Open a business bank account for Hazeydata
- **Where:** Your bank (most have free or low-cost business accounts for sole props)
- **Cost:** $0-15/month depending on bank
- **Why:** 
  - Keeps business/personal finances separate
  - Makes tax time MUCH easier
  - Looks professional for payments
- **Bring:** Business name registration, ID, BN (if you have it)

---

## 📋 When Revenue Starts

### 4. HST Registration (Required at $30k)
- [ ] Register for HST when revenue approaches $30k in 4 consecutive quarters
- **Where:** Through CRA (same portal as BN)
- **Cost:** Free to register
- **Rate:** 13% HST in Ontario
- **Note:** You can register voluntarily before $30k to claim input tax credits (ITCs) on business expenses
- **Decision:** If expenses are high (software, hosting, etc.), early registration = you get HST back on those

### 5. Invoicing Setup
- [ ] Set up professional invoicing (Wave, FreshBooks, or even just a template)
- Include: Business name, address, BN, HST number (when registered), payment terms

---

## 📊 Ongoing / Tax Time

### 6. Expense Tracking
- [ ] Set up a system to track deductible expenses:
  - **Software/SaaS:** Hosting, domains, APIs, tools
  - **Home office:** % of rent/mortgage, utilities, internet
  - **Hardware:** Computer, monitors (depreciated)
  - **Professional services:** Accountant, legal
  - **Marketing:** Ads, design, content
  - **Travel:** If attending conferences, meetings
  - **Subscriptions:** Research tools, data sources

### 7. Record Keeping
- [ ] Keep all receipts (digital is fine — use a folder or app)
- [ ] Keep records for 6 years (CRA requirement)
- **Tip:** Dedicated email folder or Google Drive folder for receipts

### 8. Tax Filing (T2125)
- [ ] Report business income/expenses on T2125 (Statement of Business Activities)
- **When:** With your personal tax return (April 30, but June 15 deadline for self-employed)
- **Payment deadline:** Still April 30 even if filing deadline is June 15
- **Consider:** Accountant for first year to set it up right

## 🔮 Future Triggers — When to Revisit

### Incorporate when:
- [ ] Annual profits exceed ~$50k consistently
- [ ] You want to leave significant money in the business (defer personal tax)
- [ ] Liability becomes a concern (contracts, customers, scale)
- [ ] You want to bring on partners/investors

### Other triggers:
- [ ] Hiring employees → Payroll account needed
- [ ] International revenue → GST/HST on exports, currency considerations
- [ ] Product sales (not just services) → Different tax treatment possible

## 📞 Professional Help

When you're ready:
- **Accountant:** Good for first tax year, incorporation decision, tax planning
- **Lawyer:** Only if incorporating or contracts become complex

**Cost-effective approach:** Do the basics yourself, pay an accountant for 1-2 hours of consultation when revenue picks up.
