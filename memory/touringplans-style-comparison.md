# TouringPlans Writing Style Comparison
*Fred vs Becky — and what it means for Hazeydata content*

---

## Side-by-Side Comparison

| Aspect | Fred | Becky |
|--------|------|-------|
| **Primary content** | Crowd Calendar Updates, methodology deep-dives | Weekly Disney Data Dump recaps |
| **Frequency** | As-needed (when data changes) | Weekly (same day every week) |
| **Structure** | Flexible based on topic | Rigid template (same sections always) |
| **Tone** | Professional-conversational | Playful-personal |
| **Person** | More "we" (TouringPlans voice) | More "I" (personal voice) |
| **Humor** | Occasional, dry | Frequent, self-deprecating |
| **Personal life** | Rarely mentioned | Regularly shared |
| **Data depth** | Deep methodology explanations | Consistent weekly metrics |
| **Reader interaction** | "Let us know in the comments" | Rock Stars, games, personal invites |
| **Accountability** | Shows error rates, explains misses | Letter grades, tracks week-over-week |

---

## What They Share

Both Fred and Becky:
- **Show the math** — Specific percentages, not vague claims
- **Admit when wrong** — Transparent about prediction misses
- **Make it actionable** — Always tie data to "what should you do?"
- **Use charts** — Visual data is central
- **Reference each other** — Becky cites Fred's updates, Fred cites Becky's recaps
- **End with engagement** — Comments, questions, feedback invited

---

## Voice Spectrum

```
FORMAL ←——————————————————————————→ CASUAL
              Fred        Becky
```

Fred is the **analyst who can explain things clearly**.
Becky is the **friend who texts you theme park updates every week**.

Both work. They serve different purposes.

---

## Content Types Best Suited For Each Style

### Fred's Style Works Best For:
- Methodology explanations
- Major calendar updates
- New park/attraction forecasts
- "Why did things change?" deep-dives
- Technical credibility pieces

### Becky's Style Works Best For:
- Weekly/recurring content
- Community building
- Newsletter voice
- Social media engagement
- "Here's what happened this week" recaps

---

## Implications for Hazeydata Content

### For the **Blog**
- Use Fred's structure for major analysis pieces
- Use Becky's consistency for any recurring series
- Both: Show the data, admit uncertainty, land on action

### For the **Newsletter**
- Lean more toward Becky's personal voice
- Include "this week's data" + "what's ahead"
- Recognize community/subscribers
- Keep a consistent template

### For the **Twitch/YouTube Show**
- Becky's energy translates well to video
- Fred's depth works for explainer segments
- Blend: Be personable AND credible

### For **Social Media**
- Becky's one-liners and hooks are tweet-ready
- Fred's specific numbers make great pull quotes
- Both: Visual data (charts) perform well

---

## The Hazeydata Voice (Proposed Blend)

**Fred's credibility + Becky's warmth**

- Data-first, always specific
- Transparent about methodology and misses
- Consistent format for recurring content
- Personal enough to feel human, professional enough to be trusted
- Community engagement built-in
- Actionable: Every piece answers "so what do I do?"
