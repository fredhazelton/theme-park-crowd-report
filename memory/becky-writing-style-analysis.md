# Becky Writing Style Analysis
*Based on Disney Data Dump articles from blog.touringplans.com (2025)*

---

## Overview

Becky writes the weekly **Disney Data Dump** series — a recurring, highly-structured crowd recap that's become a reader favorite. Her style is **playful, personal, and relentlessly consistent**. Where Fred's pieces are more analytical deep-dives, Becky's are like a weekly newsletter from a friend who happens to be obsessed with theme park data.

---

## Structure (Rigid Weekly Format)

Every Disney Data Dump follows the same template:

1. **Creative Hook** — Haiku, poem, witty opener
2. **Observed Crowd Levels** — Chart + commentary
3. **Performance of Crowd Level Predictions** — Transparency about accuracy (letter grades!)
4. **Attraction Downtime** — Overall + worst offender + rope drop issues
5. **Wait Times** — Highest by park, park-wide averages, inflation analysis
6. **Wait Time Rock Star** — Shoutout to top Lines app contributor
7. **Looking Ahead** — Weather, events, what to expect
8. **Personal Sign-off** — "Were you in the parks? Let me know!"

This consistency is *intentional* — readers know exactly what to expect each week.

## Tone & Voice

### Playful and Personal
- Uses first person liberally ("I", "my family", "we")
- Self-deprecating: "I'm jealous of you. Actually, no, that's exactly what I'm saying."
- Shares personal life: surgery recovery, Disney Cruise trips, Instagram handle
- Pop culture energy: "'Twas the week before Christmas..."

### Conversational Catchphrases
- "I said what I said."
- "See what I did there?"
- "Our favorite weekly game — guess the party dates!"
- "Welcome to your future!" (referencing the Tuesday-to-Wednesday writing delay)
- "Nope nope nope nope."
- "Gold star goes to Animal Kingdom!"

### Opinonated (in a fun way)
- "Still not worth 23 minutes. I said what I said." (on Na'vi River Journey)
- "Look, I'm not saying a Muppets theme will fix Rock'n'Roller Coaster. But at this point, it can't get much worse!"
- "This is why I personally love the week after Labor Day"

---

## Data Presentation Style

### Letter Grades for Predictions
- "We get a C+ for our predictions"
- "That means we get a D+ for our predictions"
- Simple, scannable accountability metric

### Consistent Comparisons
- Always compares to "last week" and sometimes "two weeks ago"
- "Was 65 minutes last week" format
- Tracks trends over time explicitly

### Specific Numbers with Context
- "78% of the time predictions were within 2 crowd levels"
- "11% downtime is the equivalent of the entire park shutting down for just over an hour"
- Makes percentages tangible

### Visual Callouts
- Color-coded charts (green = good, orange/red = bad)
- Image captions with personality ("At least it looks beautiful even when it's closed?")
- Clear labeling of worst offenders

---

## Community Building Elements

### Wait Time Rock Star
Every week, she calls out the top Lines app contributor by username. Creates gamification and recognition.

### "Our" Language
- "Our favorite weekly game"
- "Our current timing champion, preef"
- Creates in-group feeling

### Call to Action
Always ends with: "Were you in the parks this past week? Or are you headed to Orlando soon? Let me know in the comments below!"

### Personal Updates
- "My family and I are sailing on the Wish this weekend!"
- "If you see me, please say hello!"
- "I have a great new scar... I'm very Harry Potter chic now"

---

## Formatting Choices

- **Bold** for emphasis on key takeaways
- Bullet lists for rope drop issues, attractions
- Charts/images inline with commentary
- Consistent section headers (same every week)
- Emoji used sparingly but effectively (✨, 😉)

---

## Key Vocabulary

- "crowd level" (1-10 scale)
- "posted wait" vs "actual wait"
- "overprediction" / "underprediction"
- "downtime" / "capacity lost"
- "rope drop"
- "party days" / "non-party days"
- "headliners"
- "inflation" (wait time inflation)

## Creative Hooks (Examples)

**Haiku:**
> Last blog of the year  
> Crowds have finally arrived  
> Deep breaths, everyone

**Holiday Poem:**
> 'Twas the week before Christmas, and all through the World,  
> not a crowd was yet stirring — few strollers unfurled.

**One-liner:**
> "After a couple of weeks of orange bars and fall breaks, it's like someone hit the pause button at Walt Disney World this week."

**Dramatic:**
> "Well, that escalated quickly."

---

## What Makes Becky's Writing Work

1. **Consistency** — Same format every week builds habit and trust
2. **Personality** — You feel like you know her
3. **Transparency** — Grades predictions publicly, admits when wrong
4. **Community** — Engages readers, recognizes contributors
5. **Tangible context** — Makes percentages meaningful ("like the entire park shutting down for 2 hours")
6. **Fun** — It's data, but it doesn't feel like homework
