Legal & IP Strategy

Protect what you build before someone takes it

📜 Data Rights & Scraping Legality Critical

Every data source needs a legal assessment before relying on it in a commercial product.

  • Public school calendars: Government data, generally safe. But aggregation at scale may trigger terms-of-service issues on individual school district websites.
  • Theme park wait times: Disney, Universal, etc. actively protect this data. Scraping their apps/sites likely violates ToS. Need API partnerships or user-contributed data models.
  • Competitor data: Scraping TouringPlans, Thrill-Data, etc. is legally risky and ethically questionable. Clean-room your competitive intelligence.
  • Canada vs. US law: You operate from Canada, serve US customers, scrape US sites. Need to understand both jurisdictions.
⚡ Action: Get a 1-hour consultation with a tech/IP lawyer before launching any paid product that relies on scraped data. Budget: ~$300-500 CAD. This is the cheapest insurance you'll ever buy.

🛡️ Intellectual Property Critical

What you own, what you can protect, and what's your actual moat.

  • Prediction models: Your algorithms, training methodology, feature engineering — this is trade secret territory. Document everything, restrict access.
  • School schedule corpus: The raw data isn't protectable, but your structured, cleaned, normalized dataset is a compiled work with copyright protection.
  • The Flintstones Framework itself: The AI orchestration methodology is potentially valuable IP. Consider whether to open-source (marketing) or keep proprietary (moat).
  • Brand: "Hazeydata" trademark registration — file before someone squats it. ~$250 USD via USPTO, ~$330 CAD via CIPO.
📝 IP Register: Maintain a living document listing every IP asset, its protection status, and renewal dates. Wilma should track this.

📋 Terms of Service & Privacy Critical

Required before any user touches the product.

  • Privacy Policy: Required by law in both Canada (PIPEDA) and US (state laws). Must disclose what data you collect, how you use it, who you share it with.
  • COPPA Compliance: Your audience is families planning theme park trips. Children under 13 may use your site. COPPA violations carry $50K+ fines per violation.
  • Terms of Service: Limit liability, define acceptable use, protect your content from scraping (ironic, yes, but necessary).
  • Cookie/tracking consent: If you use analytics (you should), you need consent banners for GDPR visitors (Europeans planning Disney trips).
⚡ Don't write these yourself. Use a template service like Termly (~$10/mo) or get a lawyer to draft them once (~$500-1000). The cost of getting it wrong is catastrophic.

🏛️ Business Structure Evolution Important

Sole proprietorship → corporation as revenue grows.

  • Current: Sole proprietorship (Ontario). Fine for now — simple, cheap, fast.
  • Trigger to incorporate: When annual revenue exceeds ~$50K CAD, or when you take on any liability risk (paid users, data licensing contracts).
  • Why incorporate: Limited liability (your house isn't at risk if someone sues), tax advantages (small business deduction in Canada), ability to raise capital, professional credibility.
  • Type: Federal or Ontario corporation. Federal gives you name protection across Canada.
  • Consider US LLC later if significant US B2B revenue (simplifies invoicing and tax withholding).

🧮 Key Financial Metrics to Track Monthly

Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery

What happens when things go wrong

🚌 The Bus Factor Critical

If Fred is incapacitated, can the business survive?

  • Current bus factor: 1. If Fred can't work, everything stops. This is the #1 existential risk.
  • Mitigation — Documentation: Every process, every password, every decision rationale documented in the knowledge base. If someone else had to take over, could they?
  • Mitigation — Automation: The more the AI team handles autonomously, the less single-person dependent the business is. Wilma + Bam-Bam can keep the pipeline running without Fred for weeks.
  • Mitigation — Succession plan: Identify one trusted person (Chantale?) who knows where the keys are. Not to run the business — just to keep the lights on.
  • Mitigation — Dead man's switch: If the server goes unattended for 7 days, automated email to designated person with access instructions.
⚡ Create a "Break Glass" document: one page with all critical access info (server credentials, domain registrar, payment processor, bank info, Backblaze keys) stored securely offline. Give Chantale the location and the passphrase.

💾 Data Backup Strategy Important

Backblaze is good — but backup ≠ business continuity.

  • Backblaze B2: ✅ Great for file-level backup. Cost-effective. Reliable. Keep this.
  • What Backblaze doesn't cover:
  • Application state (running processes, cron jobs, service configs)
  • Database consistency (backing up files while DB is writing = possible corruption)
  • Recovery time — restoring from Backblaze to a new server could take days
  • Configuration drift — is the backup a complete, bootable system?
Recommended backup layers:
Layer 1: Backblaze B2 — file backup (daily) ✅ already have
Layer 2: GitHub — all code repos (continuous) ✅ already have
Layer 3: Database dumps — nightly pg_dump / sqlite backup to Backblaze ⚠️ verify
Layer 4: Server snapshot — monthly full system image (Clonezilla or similar) 🔴 add this
Layer 5: Recovery runbook — step-by-step "rebuild from scratch" document 🔴 add this

🔌 Technology Dependency Risks Important

  • Apple Silicon bet: If Apple discontinues Mac Studio or MLX breaks — you still have cloud API fallback. Cost goes up, but business doesn't die.
  • LLM API pricing: If Anthropic/OpenAI 10x prices — Tier 4 local LLM is your hedge. The Mac Studio pays for itself in ~6 months of avoided API costs.
  • Data source fragility: School district websites change constantly. Build scrapers that fail gracefully and alert, not crash silently. Maintain a "last known good" cache.
  • Domain/DNS: Where is hazeydata.ai registered? Set calendar reminders for renewal. Domain loss is business-ending.
  • Single server risk: wilma-server dies = everything is down. Plan: failover to cloud (DigitalOcean droplet with pre-configured image) within 4 hours.

Technical Architecture & Scalability

From 100 users to 100,000 without rebuilding

📐 Architecture Phases Staged

Phase 1 — Solo Server (Now → 1K users)

wilma-server handles everything: pipeline, API, website. Simple. Fast to iterate. Fine for early traction.

Phase 2 — Split Workloads (1K → 10K users)

Static site on CDN (Cloudflare Pages). API on dedicated server/VPS. Pipeline stays on wilma-server. Database on managed service (PlanetScale, Supabase).

Phase 3 — Scale Out (10K → 100K users)

API behind load balancer. Caching layer (Redis). CDN for all static assets. Background job queue for heavy computation. Consider serverless for API (Cloudflare Workers = cheap at scale).

Phase 4 — Enterprise (100K+ users)

Multi-region deployment. Dedicated B2B API tier with SLA guarantees. Data warehouse for analytics. This is the "nice problem to have" phase.

⚡ Uptime & SLA Planning Important

  • Free tier SLA: "Best effort." No guarantees. Status page for transparency.
  • Pro tier SLA: 99.5% uptime (~3.6 hours downtime/month). This is achievable with a single well-managed server + CDN.
  • API/Business SLA: 99.9% uptime (~43 min downtime/month). Requires redundancy. Don't promise this until Phase 2 architecture.
  • Monitoring: UptimeRobot (free) or Better Uptime ($20/mo). Alert on downtime within 1 minute. Wilma can handle automated recovery for common failures.
🎯 Start with a public status page from day one. Even if you're small, it signals professionalism. Instatus or Cachet (self-hosted, free).

🔌 API Architecture Design Now

Design the API before you need it — so it's ready when B2B comes knocking.

  • RESTful JSON API with versioning (v1/parks, v1/predictions, v1/schools)
  • API key authentication with rate limiting per tier
  • Endpoints: parks list, crowd predictions (date + park), school schedules (district + dates), historical data (date range)
  • Response format: standardized JSON with metadata (confidence scores, data freshness, model version)
  • Documentation: auto-generated from OpenAPI spec (Swagger UI)
  • SDK/client libraries: Python, JavaScript (when demand warrants)

🗄️ Data Architecture Important

  • Crown jewels (protect at all costs):
    • School schedule corpus (normalized, structured)
    • Historical crowd predictions + actuals
    • ACCORD engine parameters + training data
    • User behavior data (anonymized)
  • Data pipeline: Raw scrape → Clean → Normalize → Enrich → Store → Serve
  • Versioning: Every model version, every dataset version tagged. Ability to roll back predictions to any point in time.
  • Data retention policy: How long do you keep user data? Raw scrapes? Define this now, enforce later.

🌍 Data Ethics Framework Important

You're tracking how families travel. That comes with responsibility.

  • Minimize collection: Only collect what you need. You don't need names, addresses, or children's ages to predict crowd levels.
  • Anonymize everything: User behavior data should be aggregated, never individual. No "user #4521 visited Disney on March 15."
  • Transparency: Publish your data practices. "Here's exactly what we collect and why." Make it readable, not legalese.
  • No dark patterns: No tricking people into paid tiers. No "your trip will be ruined without Pro!" fear-mongering. Let the data speak.
  • School schedule data: This is about school systems, not individual students. Never collect or display data that could identify a specific school's students.

🔑 The ACCORD engine and school schedule methodology are the portable assets. Once proven for Florida, applying them to Paris or Tokyo is engineering work, not research work.

Brand Guidelines

Consistent brand = trust at scale

🎨 Visual Identity Define with Pebbles

  • Logo: Primary, icon-only, dark/light variants. Pebbles to design. Must work at 16px (favicon) and 512px (social).
  • Color palette: Define primary, secondary, accent, success, warning, error colors. Document hex codes.
  • Typography: Heading font, body font, code font. Web-safe + fallbacks.
  • Imagery style: Data visualizations, not stock photos. Show the intelligence, not generic theme park images.
  • Social templates: Instagram post, Twitter card, Discord embed — all pre-designed for consistency.

📝 Content Guidelines Define with Betty

  • Writing style: AP style with exceptions (Oxford comma = yes. "Data" = singular. "Theme park" = two words.)
  • Park naming: "Walt Disney World" on first reference, "Disney World" thereafter. Never "WDW" in user-facing content.
  • Numbers: Always use specific numbers, not vague terms. "Crowd level 7/10" not "moderate crowds."
  • Confidence language: "We predict" (high confidence) vs. "We estimate" (medium) vs. "Early indications suggest" (low). Match language to model confidence.
  • Social handles: @hazeydata everywhere. Consistent. Already claimed?
