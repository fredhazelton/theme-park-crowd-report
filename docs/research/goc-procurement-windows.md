# Government of Canada AI Procurement Windows & Processes

## Executive Summary

This report provides comprehensive guidance for hazeydata.ai on pursuing Government of Canada (GoC) contracts for AI tools and software. The Canadian government has established specific procurement frameworks for AI services, with multiple pathways available through Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC).

**Key Opportunities:**
- AI Source List qualification provides streamlined access to government AI contracts
- TBIPS framework offers entry point for IT contracts under $3.75M 
- Q4 fiscal year (Jan-March) presents highest procurement activity
- Pre-qualification processes reduce proposal development time by 60-75%

## 1. Procurement Cycles and Timing Windows

### Federal Fiscal Year Structure
- **Fiscal Year:** April 1 - March 31
- **Q1:** April - June
- **Q2:** July - September  
- **Q3:** October - December
- **Q4:** January - March

### Critical Timing Windows

#### Budget Planning Cycle
- **February/March:** Federal budget announced (before fiscal year start)
- **April-June (Q1):** Departmental budget allocation and planning
- **July-September (Q2):** Initial procurement planning and RFSO releases
- **October-December (Q3):** Peak RFP activity as departments spend allocated budgets
- **January-March (Q4):** Fiscal year-end spending surge - highest procurement activity

#### TBIPS Qualification Cycles
- **Historical Pattern:** Quarterly refresh cycles ending on last business day of March, June, September, and December
- **Current Status:** Schedules may vary; monitor PSPC announcements
- **Recommendation:** Prepare applications 6-8 weeks before quarter-end deadlines

### Optimal Entry Points
1. **September-November:** Submit TBIPS/Standing Offer qualifications
2. **January-March:** Respond to peak RFP activity during fiscal year-end
3. **April-June:** Network with departments planning next year's procurements

## 2. Standing Offers vs Competitive Processes

### Standing Offers (RFSO - Request for Standing Offer)
**Advantages:**
- Pre-qualified supplier status
- Reduced proposal development time (60-75% savings)
- Multi-year agreements (typically 12 months, renewable)
- Bypass competitive bidding for individual call-ups
- Recurring revenue opportunities

**Process:**
1. PSPC releases RFSO for specific categories
2. Suppliers submit qualifications
3. Pre-qualified suppliers receive Standing Offer agreements
4. Government departments issue "call-ups" for specific requirements
5. Call-ups may be competitive among pre-qualified suppliers or direct awards

### Competitive Processes (RFP - Request for Proposal)
**When Used:**
- Unique/complex requirements not covered by Standing Offers
- High-value contracts (typically >$3.75M)
- Specialized AI solutions requiring custom development

**Timeline:**
- Typical RFP process: 6-12 weeks from release to contract award
- Evaluation period: 4-6 weeks
- Contract negotiation: 2-4 weeks

## 3. AI-Specific Requirements and Considerations

### AI Source List - Primary Pathway
**Overview:** PSPC maintains a pre-qualified list of AI suppliers across three categories:
1. **Insights and Predictive Modeling**
2. **Machine Interactions**  
3. **Cognitive Automation**

**Current Status:**
- Ongoing qualification process
- Open Invitation to Qualify available on CanadaBuys
- Next major refresh expected before September 2026

#### Qualification Requirements
- Demonstrate capability in one or more AI categories
- Provide evidence of responsible AI practices
- Meet security clearance requirements (varies by project)
- Financial stability documentation
- Canadian business registration

#### Ethical AI Standards
Government emphasizes "responsible and effective AI services" requiring:
- Bias detection and mitigation protocols
- Data privacy and security compliance
- Transparency in AI decision-making processes
- Alignment with Treasury Board Secretariat AI guidelines

### Security Considerations
- **Enhanced security screening** for AI projects involving sensitive data
- **Privacy Impact Assessments** mandatory for AI implementations
- **Indigenous partnership requirements** may apply to certain projects

## 4. Budget Planning Timelines

### Departmental Budget Cycles
**Annual Planning (April-June):**
- Departments receive budget allocations
- Strategic planning for major AI initiatives
- Multi-year project planning

**Quarterly Reviews:**
- Q1 (April-June): Initial allocation and planning
- Q2 (July-Sep): Mid-year adjustments and procurement planning
- Q3 (Oct-Dec): Active procurement and project initiation
- Q4 (Jan-Mar): Fiscal year-end spending and project completion

### Procurement Value Thresholds
- **Under $25,000:** Low dollar value - direct quotes possible
- **$25,000-$100,000:** Competitive quotes required
- **$100,000-$3.75M:** TBIPS framework or competitive RFP
- **Over $3.75M:** Major competitive procurement process

## 5. Key Procurement Vehicles

### TBIPS (Task-Based Informatics Professional Services)
**Scope:** IT contracts under $3.75M with task authorizations capped at $1.5M
**Benefits:**
- Pre-qualified supplier pool
- Decentralized procurement (departments procure directly)
- Reduced administrative burden
- Faster contract execution

**Categories Relevant to AI:**
- Business Intelligence and Analytics
- Data Management and Analytics
- Software Development and Integration
- Technical Architecture and Design

### AI Source List
**Purpose:** Streamlined procurement of AI services, solutions, and products
**Access:** Direct departmental procurement without separate competitive process
**Scope:** All AI-related services from basic analytics to advanced machine learning

### PSPC Centralized Procurement
**For:** High-value, complex, or government-wide AI initiatives
**Process:** Traditional RFP with extended evaluation periods
**Timeline:** 6-12 months from planning to contract award

## 6. Documentation and Proposal Requirements

### Standard Documentation (All Processes)
1. **Corporate Profile**
   - Business registration and corporate structure
   - Financial statements (2-3 years)
   - Insurance coverage certificates
   - Security clearance certificates (if required)

2. **Technical Capability**
   - Portfolio of relevant AI projects
   - Team qualifications and certifications
   - Technical approach and methodologies
   - Infrastructure and security protocols

3. **Past Performance**
   - Reference letters from previous clients
   - Case studies demonstrating AI expertise
   - Performance metrics and success stories

### AI Source List Specific Requirements
1. **AI Ethics Framework**
   - Bias detection and mitigation procedures
   - Transparency and explainability protocols
   - Data governance and privacy protection

2. **Technical Demonstrations**
   - Proof of concept presentations
   - Live system demonstrations
   - Technical architecture documentation

### TBIPS-Specific Requirements
- **Resource Validation:** Proof of consultant consent and resume verification
- **Category-Specific Experience:** Project summaries aligned with TBIPS categories
- **89 Mandatory Response Elements** as outlined in TBIPS Model Bid Solicitation
