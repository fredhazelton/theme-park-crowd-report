# SSD Competitive Intelligence Report
## School Schedules Database (SSD) Competitive Analysis

**Date:** March 19, 2026  
**Scope:** North American Market Focus  
**Analyst:** Clawdbot Research Team

---

## Executive Summary

The school calendar and educational data market features a mix of direct competitors offering school schedule data, adjacent competitors providing broader education datasets, and holiday/calendar API providers serving general market needs. Key findings reveal pricing models ranging from free tiers to enterprise solutions exceeding $30,000 annually, with significant market gaps in comprehensive, real-time school calendar data specifically for business intelligence applications.

---

## 1. Direct Competitors (School Calendar/Schedule Data Providers)

### 1.1 PredictHQ
**Company Overview:** Event intelligence platform with school holidays as a key data vertical

**Key Offerings:**
- School holidays API covering 11,000+ US school districts
- District-level granularity with predicted student impact numbers
- Coverage includes summer, spring, winter, and holiday breaks
- Global coverage: US at district level, UK at county level, 14 major countries at region level, 56 countries at national level

**Data Quality & Coverage:**
- High-quality, comprehensive event data with intelligence layer
- Real-time updates and historical data
- Ranked event data with predicted attendance and impact metrics
- 19 major event categories including school holidays

**Delivery Methods:**
- RESTful API with OAuth 2.0 authentication
- Events API and Features API for model integration
- Real-time and batch processing capabilities
- Integration with existing forecasting and BI tools

**Pricing Model:**
- Tiered pricing structure (specific pricing not publicly disclosed)
- Custom enterprise pricing available
- Free data comparison service offered

**Market Positioning:**
- Premium event intelligence platform
- Target market: Large enterprises, demand forecasting, dynamic pricing
- Focus on business impact and demand intelligence

**Strengths:**
- Comprehensive event intelligence beyond just school data
- Strong enterprise focus and established customer base
- High data quality with predictive analytics
- Global coverage

**Weaknesses:**
- Primarily focused on demand forecasting use cases
- Likely expensive for smaller organizations
- Limited transparency on pricing

## 2. Adjacent Competitors (Education Data Providers)

### 2.1 SchoolDigger
**Company Overview:** Leading US school data provider since 2006

**Key Offerings:**
- API access to 130,000+ schools and 18,500+ districts
- School performance metrics, test scores, demographics
- 20 years of historical data
- Widget embeds, API access, and bulk downloads

**Data Quality & Coverage:**
- Over 20 million test score records (10 years)
- Comprehensive school directory with addresses, phone, lat/long
- Student demographics, teacher counts, spending per pupil
- SchoolDigger rankings and performance tiers

**Delivery Methods:**
- RESTful JSON API
- Copy-paste widgets (no coding required)
- Bulk CSV downloads via AWS Marketplace
- Quarterly/weekly/daily updates depending on plan

**Pricing Structure:**
- **Basic:** $19.90/month (2,000 calls included, then $0.004/call)
- **Pro:** $89.00/month (2,000 calls included, then $0.008/call)
- **Enterprise:** $189.00/month (2,000 calls included, then $0.010/call)
- **Autocomplete Only:** $9.90-$19.90/month
- **Dev/Test:** Free (1 call/minute, 20/day limit)
- **Bulk Data Downloads:** $399-$34,500/year on AWS Marketplace

**Market Positioning:**
- Comprehensive US school data authority
- Target market: EdTech companies, real estate, demographic analysis
- Focus on school performance and directory information

**Strengths:**
- Established 18-year track record
- Comprehensive US coverage with deep historical data
- Transparent pricing and multiple access methods
- Strong developer-friendly approach

**Weaknesses:**
- US-only coverage
- Limited real-time calendar/schedule data
- Focus on static school information rather than dynamic schedules

### 2.2 MCH Strategic Data
**Company Overview:** K-12 education data provider with 90+ years experience

**Key Offerings:**
- Current educator data and school rosters

**Market Positioning:**

- Target market: Education marketing companies, EdTech vendors

**Strengths:**
- Long-established industry presence (90+ years)
- Focus on timeliness and marketing applications
- Deep education sector expertise

**Weaknesses:**

- Unclear API availability
- Focus on marketing data rather than operational schedules

### 2.3 Agile Education Marketing
**Company Overview:** Educational data provider with comprehensive school coverage

**Key Offerings:**
- Data across public libraries, K-12 schools, early childhood education
- At-home educator contact information
- Higher education institution data
- Interactive dashboards for data visualization

**Market Positioning:**

**Strengths:**

## 3. Government/Public Data Sources

### 3.1 NCES (National Center for Education Statistics)
**Key Offerings:**
- Common Core of Data (CCD) - 100,000+ public schools, 18,000+ districts
- EDGE Open Data APIs for educational statistics
- Elementary/Secondary Information System (ElSi)
- DataLab tools (PowerStats, TrendStats, QuickStats)

**Data Quality & Coverage:**
- Official US Department of Education data
- Comprehensive public school coverage
- Historical and trend data available
- Standardized, authoritative data source

**Pricing:** Free (government data)

**Strengths:**
- Official, authoritative source
- Comprehensive coverage
- Free access
- Well-documented APIs

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited to statistical/demographic data
- No real-time calendar information
- Government update cycles (slower)
- US public schools only

### 3.2 Statistics Canada - Open Database of Educational Facilities (ODEF)
**Key Offerings:**
- ~19,000 educational facility records across Canada
- Centralized, harmonized repository
- Available under Open Government License

**Data Quality & Coverage:**
- All education levels and types included
- Updated periodically as new datasets become available
- Comprehensive Canadian coverage

**Pricing:** Free (government data)

**Strengths:**
- Comprehensive Canadian coverage
- Government-backed accuracy
- Open license for commercial use

**Weaknesses:**
- Limited update frequency
- Static facility data only
- No calendar/schedule information

### 4.1 Calendarific

**Key Offerings:**
- National holidays, local observances, religious events
- Worldwide coverage with multiple languages
- Historical and upcoming holiday data

**Pricing Structure:**
- **Free:** $0/month (500 calls, attribution required)
- **Starter:** $100/year or $12/month (10,000 calls)
- **Business:** $500/year (50,000 calls, weekly updates)
- **Enterprise:** $4,000/year (50M calls, daily updates)

**Data Quality & Coverage:**
- 230+ countries covered
- Updated quarterly to daily depending on plan
- Multiple languages supported
- Historical and future data available

**Delivery Methods:**
- RESTful API
- CSV/XLS data export
- Multiple API keys support
- SLA guarantees on higher tiers

**Strengths:**
- Extensive global coverage
- Clear, transparent pricing
- Multiple delivery formats
- Enterprise-ready infrastructure

**Weaknesses:**
- Generic holidays only, no school-specific data
- No district-level granularity
- Limited educational context

**Coverage:**

**Strengths:**

- Cross-referenced for accuracy

**Weaknesses:**

### 4.3 OpenHolidays API

## 6. Pricing Models and Market Analysis

### 6.1 Pricing Model Patterns

**API-Based Pricing (Most Common):**
- Base monthly/annual fee + per-call pricing
- Tiered pricing with increasing call volumes
- Example: SchoolDigger ($19.90-$189/month + $0.0028-$0.0100/call)

**Subscription Tiers:**
- Free tiers: 500-2,000 calls/month (limited features)
- Starter: $100-$144/year (10,000-50,000 calls)
- Business: $500-$1,068/year (50,000+ calls)
- Enterprise: $4,000-$34,500/year (unlimited or very high volumes)

**Bulk/Data Purchase:**
- One-time or annual licensing fees
- Range: $399-$34,500 per year
- Updated quarterly to annually

### 6.2 Market Positioning Analysis

**Premium/Enterprise Focus:**
- PredictHQ: Demand intelligence, dynamic pricing
- SchoolDigger Enterprise: Comprehensive historical data
- Calendarific Business/Enterprise: Global holiday intelligence

**Developer/SMB Focus:**
- SchoolDigger Basic/Pro: Affordable school data access
- Calendarific Starter: Basic holiday integration
- Abstract API: Simple, reliable holiday data

**Free/Open Source:**
- NCES APIs: Government educational statistics
- OpenHolidays API: Community-driven holiday data
- Free tiers from commercial providers

## 7. Market Gaps and Differentiation Opportunities

### 7.1 Identified Market Gaps

**Real-Time School Calendar Data:**
- Current providers focus on static information or annual holiday patterns
- Limited real-time updates on schedule changes, closures, early dismissals
- No comprehensive source for day-to-day operational schedules

**Granular District-Level Coverage:**
- PredictHQ covers districts but focuses on major breaks/holidays
- Missing daily schedule information, half-days, professional development days
- Limited coverage of smaller districts and rural schools

**Canadian Market Underserved:**
- Statistics Canada provides facility data but no operational calendars
- No comprehensive Canadian school calendar API identified
- Opportunity for North American coverage expansion

**SMB/Mid-Market Pricing Gap:**
- Jump from basic tiers (~$100-500/year) to enterprise (~$4,000+/year)
- Limited options for mid-market customers (school districts, regional businesses)
- Opportunity for $500-2,000/year tier with enhanced features

**Cross-System Integration:**
- Limited integration between different education data sources
- Opportunity to aggregate multiple data streams (SIS, demographics, calendars)
- Value-add through data normalization and enrichment

### 7.2 Differentiation Strategies

**Comprehensive Coverage:**
- Combine federal holidays, state/provincial holidays, and district-specific calendars
- Include both planned and unplanned schedule changes
- Cover both US and Canadian markets comprehensively

**Real-Time Updates:**
- Weather-related closures and delays
- Last-minute schedule changes
- Professional development and teacher workdays

**Business Intelligence Focus:**
- Analytics and insights beyond raw calendar data
- Demographic correlation and impact analysis
- Custom reporting and dashboard tools

**Flexible Pricing:**
- Fill the mid-market gap with competitive pricing
- Usage-based pricing for variable demand
- Regional/district-specific pricing options

**Enhanced Data Quality:**
- Multiple source verification
- Automated change detection and notifications
- Historical trending and pattern analysis

**Developer Experience:**
- Superior API documentation and SDKs
- Webhook support for real-time notifications
- Multiple output formats and integration options

## 8. Competitive Strengths and Threats

### 8.1 Market Threats

**PredictHQ Expansion:**
- Well-funded with enterprise customers
- Could expand beyond holidays to full calendar data
- Strong technical infrastructure and market presence

**SIS Provider Integration:**
- PowerSchool, Infinite Campus could develop direct APIs
- Potential for vertical integration
- Access to real-time data at source

**Google/Microsoft Education:**
- Large tech companies with education platforms
- Could bundle calendar data with existing services
- Significant technical and financial resources

**Government Open Data Initiatives:**
- Increased availability of free government data
- Potential regulatory requirements for data sharing
- Could commoditize basic calendar information

### 8.2 Competitive Advantages

**First-Mover Advantage:**
- No comprehensive school calendar API currently exists
- Opportunity to establish market leadership
- Brand recognition and customer loyalty potential

**Focused Specialization:**
- Dedicated focus on school calendars vs. broader platforms
- Deep domain expertise and feature development
- Customer-centric product development

**North American Coverage:**
- Unique value proposition for US + Canada coverage
- Cross-border business opportunities
- Regional expertise and partnerships

**Business Intelligence Layer:**
- Add intelligence beyond raw data
- Predictive analytics and trend identification
- Integration with business workflows

## 9. Recommendations

### 9.1 Strategic Positioning

**Target Market:**
- Primary: Mid-market businesses needing school calendar intelligence
- Secondary: EdTech companies, real estate, retail/hospitality
- Enterprise: Large corporations with location-based operations

**Pricing Strategy:**
- Competitive starter tier: $50-100/year (5,000-10,000 calls)
- Mid-market focus: $300-800/year (25,000-100,000 calls)
- Enterprise custom pricing above $1,500/year

**Differentiation Focus:**
- Real-time updates and notifications
- Comprehensive North American coverage
- Business intelligence and analytics layer
- Superior developer experience

### 9.2 Product Development Priorities

**Phase 1: Core Platform**
- Basic school calendar API with US coverage
- Integration with major holiday APIs for baseline data
- Real-time update infrastructure

**Phase 2: Enhanced Features**
- Canadian market expansion
- Business intelligence dashboard
- Webhook notifications and alerts

**Phase 3: Market Leadership**
- Predictive analytics and trend analysis
- White-label and enterprise solutions
- Strategic partnerships with SIS providers

### 9.3 Go-to-Market Strategy

**Competitive Pricing:**
- Undercut SchoolDigger on calendar-specific use cases
- Offer more transparent pricing than PredictHQ
- Provide better value than general holiday APIs for education use cases

**Developer-First Approach:**
- Superior documentation and sample code
- Free trial with meaningful usage limits
- Active developer community and support

**Partnership Opportunities:**
- Integration partnerships with EdTech platforms
- Data partnerships with SIS providers
- Channel partnerships with education consultants
