## Product Vision

Theme Park Crowd Report is a **free, community-oriented** service that helps people plan better theme park visits using ML-powered crowd predictions and stunning visual data. The product grows organically through word-of-mouth, driven by genuinely helpful content that people want to share.

**Core principles:**

- **Free forever during beta.** No paywall, no catch. When premium tiers arrive, the free tier remains generous.
- **Community, not broadcast.** Members are participants, not subscribers. Their feedback directly shapes what we build.
- **Ever-improving.** Every interaction teaches us something. Usage patterns and feedback drive the roadmap.
- **Stunning visuals.** Our charts, heatmaps, and crowd reports should be beautiful enough that people screenshot and share them. Visual quality is a growth engine.
- **Organic growth.** No paid acquisition. The product is the marketing. If someone shares a crowd report or a bot answer, that's the best ad we could ever run.

## Domain 1: Communication Governance

**Audit finding:** No governance layer between automated systems and customer-facing channels. Any script with the bot token could post anything to any channel at any frequency. This caused the 65-message spam incident.

### Rule 1: The Customer Channel Gate

**Nothing posts to a customer-facing channel without passing through a governance check.** This is the single most important rule in this spec.

Three tiers of customer communication:

**Tier A — Human-authored (no automation):**
Posts written and sent by Fred (or a designated human). Used for: announcements, responses to feedback, community engagement.
- No approval gate needed — Fred is the approver.
- Examples: product update announcements, welcome messages, feedback responses.

**Tier B — Scheduled automation (predictable, tested):**
Posts generated by cron jobs on a fixed schedule with tested templates. Used for: daily crowd reports, weekly summaries.
- **Approval gate:** Template reviewed and approved by Fred before first deployment. Any template change requires re-approval.
- **Quality gate:** Post content validated before sending (not empty, not error text, reasonable length).
- **Failure mode:** If quality gate fails, skip the post and alert internally. Never post garbage to customers.
- Examples: daily crowd report embed, future weekly digest.

**Tier C — Reactive automation (event-driven):**
Posts triggered by system events. Used for: service status updates.
- **Approval gate:** Script design approved by Fred (Amendment 004 process). Template approved.
- **Quality gate:** Debounce (3 consecutive checks), rate limit (max 1 new message per 6 hours), edit-in-place.
- **Failure mode:** If quality gate fails, alert internally only. Never fire customer messages on uncertain signal.
- **NO script may post to #announcements without debounce + rate limiting.** This is inviolable.
- Examples: service status transitions (per Amendment 004).

### Rule 2: Channel Permissions

| Channel | Bot can post | Bot can edit own | Automation tier allowed |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| #general | No | No | None — human only |
| #announcements | Yes | Yes | Tier A + Tier C (with Amendment 004 safeguards) |
| #crowd-reports | Yes | Yes | Tier B (daily scheduled reports) |
| #bot-commands | Yes (responses) | Yes | Tier B (command responses only — not proactive) |
| #feedback | No | No | None — human only |

The bot should never proactively post to #general or #feedback. Those are community spaces where the human voice matters.

### Rule 3: Error Visibility

**No raw error text in customer channels.** Every customer-visible error must be:
- Written in plain language (not stack traces, not "Something went wrong")
- Actionable (tell the user what to do: try again, try a different date, use a different command)
- Logged internally with full diagnostic detail

## Domain 2: Service Status Redesign

**Incorporated by reference:** `docs/V4_AMENDMENT_004_SERVICE_STATUS_REDESIGN.md`

The Amendment 004 design is the governing document for service status monitoring. Key principles summarized here for completeness:

- **Silence is golden.** Customers only hear about genuine, sustained outages.
- **Observe, don't intervene.** The monitor is a thermometer, not a thermostat. No auto-fixing.
- **One message per incident.** Edit in place. Never spam.
- **Debounce:** 3 consecutive failed checks (≥15 min) before announcing.
- **Rate limit:** Max 1 new announcement per 6 hours.
- **WAL files are normal.** DuckDB WAL presence is never treated as a problem.

**Implementation sequence:**

2. Fix WAL logic in `tpcr_bot_health_check.py` (Dino)

## Domain 3: Bot Error Handling Standards

**Audit finding:** Generic "Something went wrong querying the data" messages visible to customers. No diagnostic info, no retry suggestion, no context.

### Error Response Framework

Every bot error response must follow this pattern:

```
[Friendly acknowledgment] + [What happened in plain language] + [What to try]
```

**Error categories and responses:**

| Error Type | Current Message | New Standard |
|-----------|----------------|--------------|
| DuckDB lock/busy | "Something went wrong querying the data" | "Our database is momentarily busy — I'm retrying your question now. Hang tight!" (then auto-retry) |
| No forecast data for date | "I don't have forecast data for [park] on [date]" | "I don't have forecast data for [park] on [date] yet — our predictions currently cover [date range]. Try a date within that window, or check back soon!" |
| Park closed on requested date | (varies) | "[Park] appears to be closed on [date]. Here are the parks that are open that day: [list]. Want me to check one of those instead?" |
| API/network failure | "Something went wrong" | "I hit a temporary glitch — please try your question again in a moment. If it keeps happening, drop a note in #feedback and we'll look into it." |
| Unrecognized park/attraction | (varies) | "I'm not sure which park or attraction you mean by '[input]'. Here are the parks I cover: [list]. Could you try again with one of those?" |

### Auto-Retry Protocol

The existing auto-retry on DuckDB lock is good — keep it. Extend the pattern:

1. On transient error (DB lock, timeout), retry once automatically with a 3-second delay.
2. If retry succeeds, post the answer with: "Sorry about the brief delay — here's your answer!"
3. If retry fails, post the friendly error message from the table above.
4. Log every error (including retried-and-succeeded) internally for tracking.

### Error Logging

Every customer-visible error gets logged to a file (`logs/bot_errors.json`) with:
- Timestamp
- User ID (anonymized in reports)
- Command used
- Error type
- Error detail (internal — full stack trace)
- Resolution (auto-retried, shown error, etc.)

This feeds Domain 6 (feedback tracking) and Domain 8 (Gazoo monitoring). We can't fix what we don't measure.

## Domain 4: Daily Report Reliability

**Audit finding:** 10-day gap in #crowd-reports (Mar 26 – Apr 5) with no customer communication and no internal alerting.

### Daily Report Contract

The daily crowd report is a **Tier B scheduled automation** (Domain 1). It posts to #crowd-reports every day at a fixed time.

**Schedule:** 11:00 AM ET daily (after the 6-8 AM pipeline window completes and accuracy is computed).

**Content standard:** The crowd report embed should be visually compelling — this is the content people screenshot and share. It's a growth vector.

**Required elements:**
- Park-by-park crowd level for the day (WTI scores with color coding)
- Visual design that is clean, branded, and share-worthy
- Date clearly displayed
- Link to full forecast on hazeydata.ai

**Quality gate before posting:**
1. Data is from today's pipeline run (not stale)
2. All 4 WDW parks have scores (no missing parks)
3. WTI values are within reasonable bounds (1-70)
4. Embed renders correctly (not empty, not error text)

If any check fails: **do not post.** Alert internally to `#wti-pipeline`. A missing report is better than a broken one.

### Gap Detection

**New rule:** If #crowd-reports has no post for 24 hours, Gazoo flags it as a HIGH finding. If it reaches 48 hours, Gazoo escalates to Fred.

A 10-day gap should never happen again without someone knowing about it.

### Gap Communication

If reports will be interrupted for a known reason (maintenance, pipeline redesign), post a brief note to #announcements:

> "Daily crowd reports are paused for [reason]. We'll be back [timeframe]. Your forecasts on hazeydata.ai are still updating normally."

This takes 30 seconds and prevents customers from wondering if the service died.

## Domain 6: Feedback Tracking

**Audit finding:** 17 messages in 5 weeks. No systematic tracking. Chela's Epic Universe issue (Mar 3) still unresolved 33 days later. No proactive solicitation.

### Feedback Capture Pipeline

Every actionable feedback item in #feedback gets tracked. The flow:

```
Customer posts in #feedback
  → Fred (or Barney) reads it
  → If actionable: file a GitHub issue in the appropriate repo
    → Tag with "customer-feedback" label
    → Reference the Discord message (user + date + channel)
  → Respond to the customer in #feedback acknowledging receipt
  → When resolved: respond again in #feedback closing the loop
```

**Key rule: Close the loop.** The customer who reported the issue should hear back when it's fixed. Not a generic announcement — a direct response to their message. "Hey [name], the Epic Universe issue you flagged is fixed now — try /ask again and let us know!"

### Feedback Labels (GitHub)

| Label | When to use |
|-------|-------------|
| `customer-feedback` | Any issue originating from a customer report |
| `customer-reported-bug` | Customer found a bug |
| `customer-feature-request` | Customer suggested a feature |

### Retroactive Cleanup

File GitHub issues for known unresolved feedback:
1. **Epic Universe not included in Universal Orlando results** (Chela, Mar 3) — check if fixed; if not, file ticket
2. **Bot test message in #bot-commands** (Mar 8) — investigate and clean up

### Proactive Feedback Solicitation

**Monthly check-in:** Once per month, Fred posts a casual question in #feedback or #general. Not a formal survey — a conversation starter.

Examples:
- "What feature would make TPCR more useful for your next trip?"
- "Anyone planning a trip soon? What parks and dates? I can show you what our predictions look like."
- "We just shipped [feature]. What do you think?"
- "What's the one thing about crowd planning that we don't help with yet?"

**Timing:** Post during peak engagement hours (evenings ET, weekends). Not on a rigid schedule — organic, when it feels right.

### Usage Tracking (Future — Phase 2)

Track which bot commands are used most, which parks are queried most, which date ranges are most popular. This data drives the roadmap without asking customers directly. Implementation is a separate ticket — this spec establishes the principle that usage data informs product decisions.

## Domain 8: Gazoo Customer Experience Domain

**Audit finding:** Gazoo audits cover pipeline, scraper, competition, infrastructure, analytics, data hub, and bot health — but nothing about what customers actually experience.

### New Audit Domain: Customer Experience

Add a "Customer Experience" domain to Gazoo's audit scope with the following checks:

**Check 1: #crowd-reports freshness**
- Query: Is there a message in #crowd-reports from today?
- Scoring: Message today = pass. No message today = WARNING. No message in 48h = HIGH.

**Check 2: #announcements integrity**
- Query: Read last 10 messages in #announcements. Any message from the bot that isn't in the approved template list?
- Scoring: All messages expected = pass. Unexpected bot message = HIGH (potential spam recurrence).

**Check 3: #feedback response time**
- Query: Read last 20 messages in #feedback. Any customer (non-bot, non-Fred) message with no response within 48 hours?
- Scoring: All responded = pass. Unanswered >48h = WARNING. Unanswered >7d = HIGH.

**Check 4: Bot error rate**
- Query: Read `logs/bot_errors.json` (once Domain 3 error logging is implemented). Count errors in last 24h.
- Scoring: <5 errors = pass. 5-20 = WARNING. >20 = HIGH.

### Scoring

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 9-10 | All checks pass, daily reports posting, feedback responded to |
| 7-8 | Minor gaps (one missed day, one slow feedback response) |
| 5-6 | Multi-day report gap OR multiple unanswered feedback items |
| 3-4 | Customer-facing errors OR spam OR extended outage with no communication |
| 1-2 | Active spam incident OR complete service failure with no customer communication |

### Implementation

This requires updating Gazoo's `AUDIT_SCOPE.md` in the operations repo to add the new domain. Gazoo reads the TPCR customer channels the same way Barney does — via Discord MCP. The customer experience checks join the existing 2 AM / 4 PM audit cycle.

## Domain 9: Announcement Cadence

**Audit finding:** 4 real announcements in 5 weeks, then 28 days of silence. Significant product improvements shipped with zero customer communication.

### Cadence: Biweekly Product Update

Every two weeks, post a short, engaging update to #announcements covering what shipped. Not a changelog — a story.

**Format:**

```
🚀 **What's New — [Date Range]**

[2-3 sentences about the most impactful improvement, written for a non-technical audience]

[1-2 bullet points about other improvements]

[Optional: call to action — try a new command, check out a new page, give us feedback]
```

**Tone:** Casual, enthusiastic, human. Not corporate. Not technical. Write it like you're texting a friend who's into theme parks.

**Example:**

```
🚀 **What's New — March 22 to April 5**

Our prediction engine is now competing against itself to get smarter.
We set up a system where new forecasting approaches run side-by-side
with our current model, and only get promoted if they actually beat it
on real data. First challenger is already showing promising results.

▸ Daily recap blog posts now live at hazeydata.ai — park-by-park
  accuracy scorecards every morning
▸ Tweet automation: predicted WTI at 4 PM, actual results at 8:30 AM
  next day — follow @DisneyStatsWhiz

Fixed a monitoring bug that caused notification spam yesterday. Sorry
about that! Details in #announcements above.

What feature would be most useful for your next trip? Drop us a note
in #feedback! 🎢
```

**Ownership:** Fred writes these (Tier A — human-authored). Barney can draft them, but Fred reviews and posts. The voice should be Fred's — it's a personal product, and the community responds to that.

**Schedule:** Aim for every other Friday. If nothing meaningful shipped, skip it. A forced update with nothing to say is worse than silence. But if 3+ weeks pass with no update, post something — even "we've been heads-down on [thing], more soon."

### What Gets Announced

| Announce | Don't Announce |
|----------|---------------|
| New parks or attractions added | Internal pipeline refactors |
| Accuracy improvements (in plain terms) | Model competition results (too technical) |
| New bot commands or features | Bug fixes that customers never saw |
| Visual/design improvements | Infrastructure changes |
| Milestones (100 members, 1 year, etc.) | Agent architecture changes |
| Customer-requested features shipped | Cron schedule adjustments |

**Principle:** Announce things customers can see, use, or feel. Don't announce things that only matter internally.

## Visual Quality Standards

Fred's directive: "stunning visuals that are truly helpful." This applies to every visual the customer sees.

### Where Visuals Appear

1. **#crowd-reports daily embed** — The flagship visual. Must be share-worthy.
2. **Bot command responses** — Charts, tables, heatmaps in `/crowd`, `/today`, `/ask` responses.
3. **hazeydata.ai** — The website's year-view heatmaps, park pages, daily recap blog.
4. **Twitter (@DisneyStatsWhiz)** — Daily WTI tweets with visual data.

### Visual Principles

- **Clean over cluttered.** Every element earns its space. No decoration without function.
- **Color with meaning.** Green = low crowds, red = high crowds. Consistent everywhere. Never use color only for decoration.
- **Mobile-first.** Most Discord users are on phones. Embeds must be legible at mobile resolution.
- **Branded.** Consistent style across all touchpoints. A crowd report should be recognizable as TPCR whether it's on Discord, Twitter, or the website.
- **Shareable.** Design for the screenshot. If someone crops a crowd report and sends it to a friend, it should make sense without context.

### Visual Quality Gate

Before any new visual format ships to customers:
1. Fred reviews on both desktop and mobile
2. Screenshot test: does it make sense cropped out of context?
3. Accessibility: readable without relying solely on color (add labels or patterns)

**Pebbles** (design agent) owns visual design execution. Fred approves. Barney reviews for data accuracy.

## Growth Strategy

Organic growth means the product is the marketing. Every customer touchpoint is a potential growth moment.

### Growth Vectors

**Shareability:** Every visual output should be designed to look great when shared. Crowd report embeds, bot responses, tweet graphics, website heatmaps — these are all content that people share with friends planning Disney trips.

**Twitter presence:** @DisneyStatsWhiz posts daily predicted and observed WTI. This is public content that reaches beyond the Discord community. Visual quality of tweets directly impacts follower growth.

**Word of mouth:** When a member uses `/ask` and gets a genuinely helpful answer for their trip, they tell friends. The bot's answer quality is the highest-leverage growth investment.

**SEO (hazeydata.ai):** The daily recap blog and park forecast pages are indexed. People searching "how crowded is Magic Kingdom this week" should find us.

### What We Don't Do

- No paid ads. No influencer partnerships (unless organic). No growth hacks.
- No dark patterns (forced sharing, gated content, "invite 3 friends to unlock").
- No bots that auto-post to other servers or DM people.
- No DISBOARD or server listing services (remove the existing one).

### Growth Metrics (Track, Don't Optimize)

| Metric | Where | Frequency |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Discord member count | Server settings | Monthly |
| Bot command usage | Error/usage log (Domain 3) | Weekly |
| Twitter followers | @DisneyStatsWhiz | Monthly |
| Website visits | hazeydata.ai analytics | Weekly |
| Feedback messages | #feedback channel | Monthly |

Track these to understand trends. Don't set growth targets — the goal is to make the product better, not to hit a number.
