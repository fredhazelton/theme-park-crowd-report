# State Department of Education Research — School Calendar Rules

## Summary
Researched all 50 states + DC for school calendar mandates, typical start/end dates, and centralized calendar resources. This data was used to build state-level defaults for districts without individual calendar data.

## Key Findings

### States with Mandated Start Dates (Post-Labor Day)
These states legally restrict when schools can open, making their calendars predictable:

| State | Rule | 2025 Earliest Start | Source |
|-------|------|-------------------|--------|
| Virginia | "Kings Dominion Law" — no start before Labor Day | Sept 2, 2025 | Virginia Code § 22.1-79.1 |
| Michigan | Schools cannot start before Labor Day | Sept 2, 2025 | MCL 380.1284b |
| Minnesota | Schools cannot begin before Labor Day (exceptions possible) | Sept 2, 2025 | MN Statute 120A.40 |
| Wisconsin | Schools cannot start before Sept 1 | Sept 1, 2025 | WI Statute 118.045 |
| Maryland | Schools must start after Labor Day | Sept 2, 2025 | Maryland Education Code |
| Iowa | Cannot start before August 23 | Aug 25, 2025 | Iowa Code 279.10 |

### States with Early Start Patterns (Late July - Early August)
| State | Typical First Day | Notes |
|-------|------------------|-------|
| Arizona | Late July (Jul 28) | Year-round options available |
| Hawaii | Late July (Jul 28) | Single statewide district |
| Georgia | Early August (Aug 4) | Most districts start first week of August |
| Tennessee | Early August (Aug 4) | Law allows no later than Aug 11 |
| Mississippi | Early August (Aug 4) | 180 days required |

### States with Mid-August Starts
Alabama, Arkansas, Colorado, Idaho, Indiana, Kansas, Kentucky, Missouri, Nebraska, Nevada, New Mexico, North Dakota, Oklahoma, Texas, Wyoming

### States with Late August / September Starts
California (variable), Connecticut, Delaware, Illinois, Maine, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New York, North Carolina, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, South Carolina, Vermont, Washington

## Detailed State Research

### California (CA)
- **Population:** 5.27M students (largest state)
- **Calendar rule:** 180 instructional days required, set by individual districts
- **Start dates:** Highly variable (some year-round schools start in July, traditional start mid-Aug to early Sep)
- **Key finding from Tavily:** "The 2025-2026 school year starts in late August and ends in June"
- **Our approach:** Use state median from 51 confirmed CA districts; ~35% coverage confirmed

### Texas (TX)  
- **Population:** 5.11M students
- **Calendar rule:** Cannot start before 4th Monday in August (unless waiver)
- **Start dates:** Most districts start Aug 18-25
- **Key finding from Tavily:** "The 2025-2026 Texas school year starts on August 19 and ends on May 21"
- **Spring break:** Typically second week of March (Mar 9-13)
- **Our approach:** State median from 46 confirmed districts + DOE-confirmed dates

### New York (NY)
- **Population:** 2.31M students
- **Calendar rule:** 180 days required
- **Key finding:** NYC DOE calendar covers ~30% of state enrollment (32 geographic districts)
  - First day: Sept 4, 2025; Last day: June 26, 2026
  - Spring break: April 2-10, 2026
  - Winter break: Dec 24 - Jan 2
- **Our approach:** NYC districts get confirmed NYC DOE calendar; others get state median

### Florida (FL)
- **Population:** 2.85M students  
- **Calendar rule:** 180 instructional days; most start in August
- **Key finding:** "The 2025-2026 Florida school year starts on August 11, 2025"
- **Our approach:** 92% confirmed coverage (32 confirmed FL districts are mostly large county systems)

### Illinois (IL)
- **Population:** 1.85M students
- **Calendar rule:** 185 days (176 instructional + 9 teacher)
- **Key finding from Tavily:** "The 2025-2026 Illinois school year starts on August 26 and ends on June 10"
- **Our approach:** 4 confirmed districts + DOE dates for inference

### Pennsylvania (PA)
- **Population:** 1.50M students
- **Calendar rule:** 180 days required; late August start typical
- **Key finding:** "The 2025-2026 Pennsylvania school year starts on August 26 and ends on June 10"
- **Our approach:** 14 confirmed districts + DOE dates

### Ohio (OH)
- **Population:** 1.53M students
- **Calendar rule:** Hours-based (455-910 hours); typically late August start
- **Key finding:** "The 2025-2026 Ohio school year starts no later than August 1"
- **Our approach:** 28 confirmed districts + state median

### Georgia (GA)
- **Population:** 1.71M students
- **Calendar rule:** 180 days; early August starts common
- **Key finding:** "The 2025-2026 Georgia school year starts August 31" (note: this may be off; most GA districts actually start early August)
- **Our approach:** 44 confirmed districts (70% coverage); strong state median

### Michigan (MI)
- **Population:** 1.22M students
- **Calendar rule:** Cannot start before Labor Day (Sept 2, 2025)
- **Key finding:** Confirmed post-Labor Day mandate
- **Our approach:** 16 confirmed districts + strong state rule

### North Carolina (NC)
- **Population:** 1.40M students
- **Calendar rule:** Cannot start before nearest Monday to Aug 26; 185 days
- **Key finding:** "Must provide at least 185 days or 1,025 hours"
- **Our approach:** 36 confirmed districts (68% coverage)

### Virginia (VA)
- **Population:** 1.26M students
- **Calendar rule:** "Kings Dominion Law" — post-Labor Day
- **Key finding:** Confirmed; typical Aug 25 start for districts with waivers
- **Our approach:** 23 confirmed districts (66% coverage)

### Maryland (MD)
- **Population:** 0.89M students
- **Calendar rule:** Post-Labor Day start
- **Key finding:** "Districts set their own calendars" but post-Labor Day
- **Our approach:** 9 confirmed districts (67% coverage, including large county systems)

### Colorado (CO)
- **Population:** 0.85M students
- **Calendar rule:** 160 days or 1056 hours
- **Key finding:** "Schools start on August 12"
- **Our approach:** 9 confirmed + Tavily data (50% confirmed coverage)

### Tennessee (TN)
- **Population:** 1.00M students
- **Calendar rule:** 180 days; start no later than August 11
- **Key finding:** "The 2025-2026 school year starts no later than August 11, 2025, and ends no later than May 23"
- **Our approach:** 20 confirmed districts (48% coverage)

### New Jersey (NJ)
- **Population:** 1.26M students
- **Calendar rule:** 180 days; September start typical
- **Key finding:** "School year starts on September 1 and ends on June 18"
- **Our approach:** 22 confirmed districts (17% coverage)

### Indiana (IN)
- **Population:** 0.98M students
- **Calendar rule:** 180 days; early/mid August start
- **Key finding:** "Starts in August and ends before Memorial Day"
- **Our approach:** 17 confirmed districts (22% coverage)

### Washington (WA)
- **Population:** 1.08M students
- **Calendar rule:** 180 days
- **Key finding:** "Starts in late August and ends in late June, 173-day academic calendar"
- **Our approach:** 17 confirmed districts (31% coverage)

### Wisconsin (WI)
- **Population:** 0.81M students
- **Calendar rule:** 180 instructional days; start no earlier than Sept 1
- **Key finding:** "Start date can be before September 1 if approved by DPI"
- **Our approach:** 5 confirmed districts (12% coverage), strong state rule

### Minnesota (MN)
- **Population:** 0.79M students
- **Calendar rule:** Post-Labor Day start mandatory; 165 student days
- **Key finding:** "Starts after Labor Day, first day August 26, 2025"
- **Our approach:** 12 confirmed districts (28% coverage), strong state mandate

### Missouri (MO)
- **Population:** 0.87M students
- **Calendar rule:** 174 days or 1044 hours; mid-August typical
- **Key finding:** "Exact start and end dates determined by local districts"
- **Our approach:** 10 confirmed districts (18% coverage)

## Methodology Notes

1. **State medians** are computed from confirmed district data within each state
2. **State DOE rules** provide first_day, last_day, and typical break dates when no confirmed data exists
3. **NYC geographic districts** (32 sub-districts of NYC DOE) all follow the same citywide calendar
4. **Winter break** is nearly universal: Dec 22 - Jan 2 (±3 days) across all states
5. **Spring break** varies the most: ranges from first week of March (TX, AZ) to late April (New England)
6. **Summer timing** is the key crowd factor: early states (AZ, GA, TN) release students in May; late states (NY, NJ, MA) in late June
