## Overview

School calendar data as a commercial product is a small but real market. Two companies currently sell structured school break data, each from a different angle. We compete on the analytics and automation layer — not on the claim that no competition exists.

## Burbio (burbio.com) — Primary Competitor

**Threat level: HIGH**

Burbio is a PreK-12 intelligence platform that bundles school calendar data with board meeting analysis, enrollment tracking, bond data, RFPs, superintendent changes, and EdTech sales signals.

**School calendar specifics:**
- District-level data mapped to zip codes, counties, and NCES IDs
- Tracks start dates, break dates, and year-end schedules
- Publishes weekly "School Tracker" newsletter with calendar-driven insights
- Serves staffing, retail, travel, AND K-12 clients (broader than previously stated)
- Updates continuously as districts publish new calendars
- Multi-year historical data
- Pricing: $3,000-$6,000/year (avg ~$4,500/yr per Vendr)

**Key finding:** Burbio publishes spring break wave charts and Labor Day shift analysis that are nearly identical to our planned value prop. They are actively selling calendar data to travel and retail, not just EdTech. Our sales strategy section 4.1 ("There Is No Direct Competition") is inaccurate and should be corrected before any customer-facing use.

**Our advantages over Burbio:**
- Enrollment-weighted aggregation ("% of students" vs "% of districts")
- API-first delivery (planned)
- Price flexibility ($99/mo entry point vs their $3K+ floor)
- Focused product (calendars only, not bundled into a platform)

**Burbio's advantages over us:**
- Established brand and client relationships in travel/retail
- Continuous updates
- Broader platform creates stickiness
- Multi-year historical data

## Inntopia School Calendar Explorer — Hospitality Focused

**Threat level: MEDIUM**

An interactive dashboard of school break data built for the travel/hospitality industry.

**School calendar specifics:**
- 3,000+ public school districts plus universities across all 50 states
- Human-vetted data, refreshed quarterly (top 500 biweekly)
- Interactive dashboard with location, date, and age filters
- YOY comparison back to 2020/21 (5+ years of history)
- Exportable to spreadsheets; interactive map
- Per-license pricing (single entity + bulk for ownership groups)

**Our advantages over Inntopia:**
- 4.5x district coverage (13,400 vs 3,000)
- Enrollment weighting
- API access (planned)
- Automated pipeline (faster refresh)
- Broader market positioning

**Inntopia's advantages over us:**
- 5+ years of historical data (we have 2)
- University data included (we don't have this)
- Human-vetted quality assurance
- Established hospitality client base
- Purpose-built UX for resort/hotel operators

## Our Honest Weaknesses

1. **Historical data** — Inntopia has 5+ years. We have 2. Takes time to close.
2. **University data** — Inntopia includes it. We don't. Add to roadmap.
3. **Brand recognition** — Both competitors have established relationships.
4. **Continuous updates** — Burbio updates continuously. We're batch-oriented.
5. **Human vetting** — Inntopia's claim is a quality signal. Our automated pipeline needs a strong quality story.

## Competitive Strategy

**Against Burbio:** Position as the specialist. Better API, better pricing flexibility, enrollment-weighted aggregation. Target buyers who want calendar data without the full Burbio platform.

**Against Inntopia SCX:** Win on coverage (4.5x), price, and API. Acknowledge their history and university data. Add university data to roadmap.

**Against "build it yourself":** The real competitor. Our pitch: "We tried. It took months and cost more than our annual subscription."
