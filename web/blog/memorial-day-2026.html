Is Memorial Day Weekend at Disney World Really That Bad? Here's What the Data Says

Every year, the same advice echoes across Disney planning forums: "Whatever you do, avoid Memorial Day weekend." It's become gospel in the theme park community — right up there with "rope drop is everything" and "don't eat at the first restaurant you see."

But here's the thing. We track actual wait time data across 12 major theme parks, and the numbers tell a more nuanced story than "just stay home." Let's dig in.

What Is the Wait Time Index (and Why Does It Matter)?

Before we get into Memorial Day specifics, a quick primer. The Wait Time Index (WTI) is the average actual wait time across all operating attractions at a given park. It's a single number that tells you how a park feels on any given day.

  • WTI under 15: You're practically walking onto rides. A dream day.
  • WTI 15–20: Comfortable. Short waits, easy touring.
  • WTI 20–25: Moderate. You'll wait, but it's manageable with a plan.
  • WTI 25–30: Busy. Prioritize your must-dos and expect queues.
  • WTI 30+: Peak. Bring patience (and maybe a portable fan).

With that scale in mind, let's look at what Memorial Day weekend actually does to Disney World.

Memorial Day Disney World Crowds: The Real Numbers

Here's what our data shows for Magic Kingdom during Memorial Day weekend in recent years:

  • Saturday (Memorial Day weekend): WTI 24–28
  • Sunday: WTI 23–27
  • Memorial Day Monday: WTI 22–25

Yes, those are elevated numbers. Magic Kingdom on a quiet February Tuesday might sit around WTI 13–16. So Memorial Day weekend is noticeably busier.

But here's the myth-busting part: Memorial Day weekend isn't even close to the busiest time of year.

Mid-July regularly pushes WTI into the 28–34 range. Christmas week? WTI 30+, easily. Spring Break peaks can rival or exceed Memorial Day, depending on when Easter falls.

Memorial Day lands firmly in the "busy but not brutal" category. It's a step up from a normal weekend — not the apocalyptic crowdscape it's made out to be.

How Memorial Day Compares to Peak Summer Crowds

This is where the data gets interesting. Many families avoid Memorial Day weekend and instead visit in mid-June or July, thinking they've dodged the bullet. But the numbers don't always support that logic.

Period Typical MK WTI Range
Memorial Day Weekend 22–28
Mid-June 20–26
Early July 26–32
Mid-July (peak) 28–34
Late August 18–23

Memorial Day weekend is often comparable to or lighter than the heart of summer. The crowds build gradually through June and peak in July when every school in the country is out. If your choice is Memorial Day weekend or the second week of July, the data actually favors the holiday weekend.

The Monday Advantage: Why Memorial Day Itself Can Surprise You

Here's a pattern we've noticed: Memorial Day Monday tends to be the lightest day of the long weekend. Many visitors check out of their hotels Sunday night or spend Monday traveling home. The result? A holiday that's supposed to be packed can actually feel more relaxed than the Saturday before it.

If you're flexible, targeting Monday for your biggest park day (especially at Magic Kingdom or Hollywood Studios) can be a smart move.

Best Time to Visit Disney World in Early Summer

If you have flexibility around Memorial Day, here's what the WTI data suggests for the best time to visit Disney World in summer:

  1. The week after Memorial Day (late May/early June): Schools aren't all out yet. WTI often dips back to the low 20s or even high teens on weekdays.
  2. Late August: Schools start returning to session across the South. Crowds thin noticeably.
  3. Memorial Day Monday itself: As noted, often the lightest day of the long weekend.
  4. Early September (after Labor Day): Technically not summer anymore, but the weather is still warm and the parks are blissfully calm.

The worst stretch? Late June through mid-July. If you're trying to beat the crowds, that's the window to avoid — not Memorial Day.

The Bottom Line on Memorial Day Weekend at Disney

Should you go to Disney World on Memorial Day weekend? The data says: it depends on your alternative.

If your options are Memorial Day weekend or a quiet week in September, sure — September wins every time. But if you're choosing between Memorial Day and mid-July, the holiday weekend is actually the better bet by the numbers.

Memorial Day has earned a reputation it doesn't fully deserve. It's busy, but it's not peak. And with the right strategy — hitting the parks on Monday, using WTI forecasts to pick your best park each day, and planning around the data instead of the myths — you can absolutely have a great trip.
